Unaccompanied Minors: A Data-Driven Visualization

Introduction

Welcome to this data visualization project exploring the journey of unaccompanied minors traveling to the United States. This project highlights key aspects such as arrival demographics, detainment, and release patterns, providing clear visual narratives supported by data-driven insights.

Arrival

This section focuses on the demographics of unaccompanied minors arriving in the United States, including:

  • Countries of origin
  • Age and gender distribution
  • Patterns of migration over time

Visualizations include charts and graphs that illustrate the trends and breakdowns of this critical stage.

Detainment

Detainment is a significant part of the journey for many unaccompanied minors. This section visualizes:

  • The average duration of detainment
  • Trends in detainment locations
  • Demographics of individuals in detainment centers

Insights are presented using bar charts, heatmaps, and time series analyses.

Release

After detainment, many minors are released to sponsors or organizations. This section covers:

  • Destinations of release (states or regions)
  • Types of sponsors (family, shelters, etc.)
  • Patterns in release over time

Visualizations explore the geographical and temporal trends of the release process.
